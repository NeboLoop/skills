# Copywriting

Write marketing copy that converts — for landing pages, homepages, pricing pages, feature pages, and product pages.

## When to Use

Activate this skill when the user says any of these:
- "Write copy for [page type]"
- "Help me with my homepage"
- "Draft copy for my landing page"
- "Write a headline for..."
- "Improve this copy"
- "Make this more compelling"
- "My copy is weak"
- "Help me describe my product"
- "Write a value proposition"
- "Create a tagline"
- "What should my CTA say"
- "Above the fold copy"
- "Hero section copy"

This skill is for website marketing copy that needs to persuade visitors to take action. For email copy, use the email-sequence skill instead. For editing existing copy, use copy-editing.

## Context Gathering

Before writing any copy, gather this information. If the user has already created product-marketing-context, recall that first from memory to avoid asking redundant questions.

Ask these questions in plain conversational language:

**1. What type of page is this?**
- Homepage (serves multiple audiences)
- Landing page (single campaign with one goal)
- Pricing page (helps people choose a plan)
- Feature page (explains one specific feature)
- Product page (selling a specific product)
- About page (tells your story)

**2. What's the ONE action you want visitors to take?**
- Start a free trial
- Book a demo
- Make a purchase
- Download something
- Sign up for a waitlist
- Contact sales
- Join an email list

**3. Who is this page for?**
- What's their role? (founder, marketer, developer, etc.)
- What problem are they trying to solve right now?
- What words do THEY use to describe this problem?
- What makes them hesitate or hold back from taking action?

**4. What are you offering?**
- What is the product or service?
- What outcome or transformation does it create?
- What makes it different from alternatives?
- Why should they believe you? (proof points: numbers, testimonials, customers)

**5. Where is traffic coming from?**
- Paid ads (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn)
- Organic search (what are they searching for?)
- Email campaigns
- Social media
- Direct (they already know about you)

This matters because it tells you what context visitors already have when they land on the page.

**6. What's the brand voice?**
- Casual and friendly vs. professional and serious
- Bold and playful vs. understated and minimal
- Technical and precise vs. accessible and simple

If product-marketing-context exists in memory, most of these answers will already be there. Only ask for page-specific details.

## Methodology

Follow this 6-step process to write effective marketing copy:

### Step 1: Load Product Context
Use Nebo's memory to recall the product-marketing-context. If it doesn't exist, recommend creating it first — that skill captures positioning, audience, differentiation, and brand voice that every marketing skill needs.

### Step 2: Identify the Conversion Goal
Based on the page type and user input, define:
- Primary goal: What's the ONE thing visitors should do?
- Secondary goal: What's the fallback action if they're not ready for the primary?
- Stage in funnel: Awareness (first time hearing about you), Consideration (comparing options), or Decision (ready to buy)

### Step 3: Map the Persuasion Flow
Every page tells a story. Map out the logical flow:
1. Hook them (headline that resonates with their problem)
2. Build credibility (social proof, logos, numbers)
3. Show you understand (describe their pain or situation)
4. Present the solution (your product/service)
5. Explain benefits (what's in it for them — outcomes, not features)
6. Remove objections (FAQ, guarantees, comparisons)
7. Ask for action (clear CTA)

Not every page needs all seven. Landing pages do. Feature pages might skip credibility if you've already built it elsewhere.

### Step 4: Write Section by Section
Start at the top and work down. Follow these section-specific guidelines:

**Above the Fold (Headline + Subheadline + CTA):**
- Headline: One sentence that communicates the core value. Use formulas like:
  - "Achieve [outcome] without [pain point]"
  - "The [category] for [specific audience]"
  - "[Question that highlights their main problem]"
- Subheadline: Expand on the headline with 1-2 sentences of specificity
- CTA button: Action verb + what they get (e.g., "Start Free Trial" not "Sign Up")

**Social Proof Section:**
- Customer logos (if you have recognizable brands)
- Key numbers (users, revenue processed, time saved, ratings)
- Short testimonials with real names and photos

**Problem/Pain Section:**
- Describe the situation your customers face
- Use their language (not your product language)
- 2-3 short paragraphs or bullet points

**Solution/Benefits Section:**
- Introduce your product as the solution
- List 3-5 key benefits (what they GET, not what it DOES)
- Each benefit: title + 1-2 sentence explanation
- Use "you" and "your" — make it about them

**How It Works:**
- 3-4 numbered steps showing how simple it is
- Reduce perceived complexity
- Each step: title + 1 sentence

**Objection Handling:**
- FAQ (5-8 common questions)
- Guarantees (money-back, cancel anytime)
- Comparisons (vs. alternatives)

**Final CTA:**
- Recap the value
- Repeat the CTA
- Add risk reversal ("Free for 14 days, no credit card required")

### Step 5: Apply Copywriting Principles
Review your draft against these rules:

**Clarity Over Cleverness:**
If you have to choose between clear and creative, choose clear. A confused visitor doesn't convert.

**Benefits Over Features:**
- Feature: "Real-time collaboration"
- Benefit: "Your whole team sees changes instantly — no more version conflicts"

**Specificity Over Vagueness:**
- Vague: "Save time"
- Specific: "Cut reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes every week"

**Customer Language Over Company Language:**
Use words your customers use. Check reviews, support tickets, or interviews if available. Use Nebo's browser to search "[product] reviews" if needed.

**One Idea Per Section:**
Each section should advance ONE argument. Don't try to say everything at once.

**Active Voice:**
"We generate reports" not "Reports are generated"

**Remove Qualifiers:**
Cut "very," "really," "almost," "quite," "just"

**No Jargon:**
If a non-technical person wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

**No Buzzwords:**
Avoid "streamline," "optimize," "innovative," "cutting-edge," "revolutionary" unless you can back them up with specifics.

### Step 6: Create Headline Alternatives
For the main headline and CTA, provide 2-3 alternatives with different angles:
- Angle A: Problem-focused ("Tired of [pain]?")
- Angle B: Outcome-focused ("Achieve [result] in [time]")
- Angle C: Curiosity-focused ("[Surprising statement]")

Explain the strategic difference between each option so the user can choose based on their audience and brand.

## Quality Checks

Before delivering copy, verify these criteria:

**1. Clarity Check**
- Can a 12-year-old understand what you're selling and why it matters?
- Is the primary CTA obvious within 3 seconds of landing?

**2. Specificity Check**
- Did you replace vague claims with specific numbers or examples?
- Are benefits concrete outcomes, not abstract concepts?

**3. Audience Alignment**
- Does the language match how the target customer talks?
- Are you addressing their actual problem, not a problem you assume they have?

**4. Voice Consistency**
- Does the tone match the brand voice defined in product-marketing-context?
- Is the formality level consistent throughout?

**5. Structure Check**
- Does each section advance the argument logically?
- Is there a clear flow from problem → solution → action?

**6. CTA Check**
- Is the CTA action-oriented (verb + benefit)?
- Is it repeated at least twice (above fold + at the bottom)?

**7. Proof Check**
- If you included statistics, customer names, or testimonials, are they real?
- Never fabricate social proof — it erodes trust and creates legal liability

**8. Jargon Check**
- Highlight any industry terms or acronyms
- If you kept them, did you explain them?

## Examples

### Example 1: SaaS Landing Page (B2B)

**Input:**
User: "I need copy for a landing page. We're launching a new project management tool for remote teams. It's different because we have async video standups built in. Target audience is startup founders managing distributed teams. Coming from a paid ad campaign."

**Output:**

### Page: Landing Page
**Goal:** Start free trial  
**Audience:** Startup founders with remote teams  

---

### Above the Fold

**Headline:**  
Run your remote team without another Zoom meeting

**Subheadline:**  
Your team records quick video standups on their own time. You watch at 2x speed and stay in sync — no calendar Tetris required.

**CTA Button:**  
Start Free Trial

**Why this works:** The headline directly addresses the pain point (too many meetings) and hints at the outcome (async communication). The subheadline explains the mechanism (async video) and benefit (no scheduling). CTA is action-oriented and low-friction (free trial).

---

### Social Proof

**Used by 200+ remote teams including:**
[Logo row: Buffer, Ghost, Zapier, Basecamp, ConvertKit]

**Why this works:** Shows recognizable remote-first companies that validate the use case.

---

### Problem Section

**Sound familiar?**

You hired talented people across three time zones. Now you spend half your day in meetings just to stay aligned.

Standups are at 7am for someone. Discussions get delayed by 24 hours. You're managing calendars instead of building product.

**Why this works:** Uses "you" language to put the founder in the scenario. Three specific pain points they'll recognize.

---

### Solution Section

**Standups that work for everyone's schedule**

Record a 2-minute video update when you're ready. Watch your team's updates at 2x speed over coffee. Comment on anything that needs discussion.

**Key benefits:**
- **No timezone compromise** — Everyone works in their peak hours
- **Searchable history** — "What did Sarah say about that API last week?"
- **Face-to-face without the calendar** — See facial expressions, tone, energy

**Why this works:** Benefits are outcome-focused (work in peak hours, find info fast) not feature-focused (has video, has search).

---

### How It Works

1. **Team records updates** — Hit record, share what you're working on, ask questions (1-3 min)
2. **You watch when ready** — Scrub through 10 videos in 15 minutes at 2x speed
3. **Comment and align** — Reply with text or video to anything that needs discussion

**Why this works:** Three simple steps reduce perceived complexity. Time specificity (15 minutes) makes it concrete.

---

### Final CTA

**Stop playing calendar Tetris with your remote team**

Start your free trial — no credit card required. Cancel anytime.

[Start Free Trial Button]

**Why this works:** Repeats the pain point, removes risk with no credit card requirement.

---

### Headline Alternatives

**Option A:** Run your remote team without another Zoom meeting  
*Rationale:* Problem-focused. Directly calls out meeting fatigue. Best if audience is actively frustrated.

**Option B:** Async standups for remote teams that actually work  
*Rationale:* Outcome-focused. Implies past solutions failed. Good if they've tried other tools.

**Option C:** Your remote team is tired of 7am standups  
*Rationale:* Empathy-focused. Puts the team's pain front and center. Founder thinks about team happiness.

### Example 2: E-commerce Product Page

**Input:**
User: "Product page for a $200 chef's knife. Target is home cooks who are serious about cooking but not professionals. They currently use cheap knives and don't know what they're missing. Traffic is coming from organic search for 'best chef knife' and Instagram ads."

**Output:**

### Page: Product Page
**Goal:** Add to cart  
**Audience:** Serious home cooks upgrading from cheap knives  

---

### Above the Fold

**Headline:**  
The last chef's knife you'll ever buy

**Subheadline:**  
Japanese steel that holds an edge for months. Balanced so perfectly it feels weightless. Guaranteed for life.

**CTA Button:**  
Add to Cart — $200

**Why this works:** Headline positions as an investment, not an expense. Subheadline hits three benefits (edge retention, balance, durability) with sensory language.

---

### Problem Section

**Why your current knife is making cooking harder**

Cheap knives need sharpening every week. They're too light, so you have to push. The handle slips when your hands are wet.

You end up with uneven cuts, sore hands, and tomatoes that look like you attacked them.

**Why this works:** Describes their current reality with specifics they'll recognize (tomato massacre).

---

### Solution Section

**What changes when you use a real knife**

**Stays sharp for months, not days**  
High-carbon Japanese steel holds an edge 3x longer than German steel. You'll sharpen it twice a year, not twice a week.

**Cuts with its own weight**  
Perfect balance point means the knife does the work. Your hand just guides. Onions? Effortless. Butternut squash? No problem.

**Feels like an extension of your hand**  
Ergonomic handle designed for wet grip. After a week, you'll forget you're holding it.

**Why this works:** Each benefit is outcome-based (less sharpening, less effort, better comfort) with specifics (3x, twice a year).

---

### How It Works

1. **Order today** — Free shipping, arrives in 3-5 days
2. **Use it for 60 days** — Cook every meal. Test it on everything.
3. **Keep it forever** — Lifetime warranty. If it ever breaks, we replace it.

**Why this works:** Reduces purchase risk with 60-day trial and lifetime warranty.

---

### Final CTA

**Upgrade your kitchen's most important tool**

$200. Lifetime warranty. Free shipping.

[Add to Cart Button]
