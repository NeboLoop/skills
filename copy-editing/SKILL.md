# Copy Editing

Polish existing marketing copy for clarity, brand voice consistency, and conversion potential through systematic multi-pass editing.

---

## When to Use

Activate this skill when the user says:
- "Review this copy"
- "Edit my page"
- "Check this email"
- "Proofread this"
- "Make this copy better"
- "This reads awkwardly"
- "Too wordy"
- "Tighten this up"
- "Copy feedback"
- "Polish this"
- "Sharpen the messaging"

Use copy-editing when the user has existing copy they want improved. For writing new copy from scratch, use the **copywriting** skill instead.

---

## Context Gathering

Before editing, ask these questions in plain language:

1. **What is this copy for?** (landing page, email, ad, social post, pricing page, etc.)
2. **What should the reader do after reading it?** (sign up, buy, click, share, reply, etc.)
3. **Are there specific issues you've noticed?** (too long, unclear message, boring, too salesy, etc.)
4. **Do you have any proof points I can reference?** (testimonials, stats, case studies, reviews, etc.)
5. **Is there anything I should NOT change?** (brand terms, specific phrases, legal language, etc.)

After gathering context, check Nebo's memory for **product-marketing-context**. If it exists, use the brand voice, customer language, and positioning to guide your edits.

---

## Methodology

Copy editing isn't about rewriting — it's about enhancing. Use this six-pass approach to systematically improve copy while preserving the core message and the author's voice.

### Pass 1: Clarity

**Goal:** Make every sentence immediately understandable.

**What to check:**
- Confusing sentence structures — if you have to read it twice, it needs work
- Jargon or insider language — replace with plain English
- Unclear pronoun references — make it obvious what "it" or "they" refers to
- Sentences trying to say too much — split complex sentences into simple ones
- Missing context — add necessary background info

**How to execute:**
1. Read through once, highlighting unclear sections (don't edit yet)
2. For each unclear section, ask: "What is this actually trying to say?"
3. Recommend specific edits that preserve the intent but improve clarity
4. Verify the edited version is immediately understandable to someone unfamiliar with the product

**Common clarity killers to watch for:**
- Abstract language instead of concrete examples
- Burying the main point in qualifications ("While it's true that many solutions exist, our unique approach leverages..." → "We do X differently:")
- Assuming reader knowledge they don't have

### Pass 2: Brand Voice Consistency

**Goal:** Ensure the copy sounds consistent from start to finish.

**What to check:**
- Shifts between formal and casual tone
- Inconsistent personality (starting friendly, becoming corporate)
- Mixing "we" and "the company" references
- Word choices that don't match the brand voice (from product-marketing-context)

**How to execute:**
1. If product-marketing-context exists in memory, recall it to understand the brand voice
2. Read the copy aloud to hear tone inconsistencies
3. Mark where the voice shifts unexpectedly
4. Recommend edits that smooth the transitions and maintain consistent personality

**After this pass:** Loop back to Pass 1 (Clarity) to ensure voice edits didn't introduce confusion.

### Pass 3: Benefits Over Features

**Goal:** Make sure every claim answers "why should I care?"

**What to check:**
- Features without benefits ("AI-powered analytics" → "AI-powered analytics that surface insights you'd miss manually — so you make better decisions in half the time")
- Claims that don't connect to the reader's life
- Technical capabilities without outcomes
- Company achievements that don't help the reader

**The "So What" test:**
For every statement, ask "Okay, so what?" If the copy doesn't answer that question, add the benefit bridge.

**How to execute:**
1. Read each claim and literally ask "so what?"
2. Highlight claims that don't answer the question
3. Add a "which means..." or "so you can..." bridge to connect features to benefits
4. Ensure benefits connect to real desires (save time, make money, reduce stress, look good, avoid risk)

**After this pass:** Loop back to Pass 2 (Voice) and Pass 1 (Clarity).

### Pass 4: Proof and Specificity

**Goal:** Support claims with evidence and make vague language concrete.

**What to check for proof:**
- Unsubstantiated claims ("trusted by thousands" → "trusted by 2,847 teams")
- "Best" or "leading" without evidence (add source or remove claim)
- Missing social proof (add testimonials, case studies, review scores, customer logos)
- Generic statements that could apply to anyone

**What to check for specificity:**
- Vague language ("improve" → "cut reporting time by 40%")
- Round numbers that feel made up ("100+ customers" → "127 customers")
- Benefits without quantification ("save time" → "save 4 hours every week")
- Outcomes without timeframes ("fast results" → "results in 14 days")

**Specificity upgrades:**

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| Save time | Save 4 hours every week |
| Many customers | 2,847 teams |
| Fast results | Results in 14 days |
| Improve your workflow | Cut your reporting time in half |
| Great support | Response within 2 hours |

**How to execute:**
1. Identify every claim that needs proof — flag unsupported assertions
2. Check if proof exists nearby in the copy (if not, ask the user for it)
3. Replace vague words with concrete numbers, timeframes, or examples
4. Remove content that can't be made specific (it's probably filler)

**After this pass:** Loop back to Pass 3 (Benefits), Pass 2 (Voice), Pass 1 (Clarity).

### Pass 5: Emotional Resonance

**Goal:** Make the reader feel something, not just understand something.

**What to check:**
- Flat, purely informational language
- Pain points mentioned but not felt ("manual processes are time-consuming" → "you're stuck copying data between spreadsheets for hours every week")
- Aspirations stated but not evoked ("grow your business" → "picture launching a new product line because you finally have time to think strategically")
- Missing emotional triggers

**Emotional dimensions to consider:**
- Pain of the current state (frustration, stress, overwhelm)
- Fear of missing out (competitors winning, opportunities lost)
- Desire for transformation (freedom, confidence, pride)
- Relief from solving the problem (peace of mind, reduced anxiety)

**How to execute:**
1. Read for emotional impact — does it move you at all?
2. Identify flat sections that should resonate emotionally
3. Add emotional texture: paint the "before" state vividly, use sensory language, reference shared experiences
4. Ensure emotion serves the message authentically (not manipulation)

**After this pass:** Loop back to Pass 4 (Proof), Pass 3 (Benefits), Pass 2 (Voice), Pass 1 (Clarity).

### Pass 6: Remove Friction Near CTAs

**Goal:** Eliminate every barrier to taking action.

**What to check:**
- Weak or unclear CTAs ("Learn more" → "Start your free trial — no credit card needed")
- Unanswered objections near the ask
- Missing trust signals (guarantees, social proof, security badges)
- Friction language ("Sign up now" vs "Get started free")
- Hidden costs or surprises in fine print

**Risk reducers to look for:**
- Money-back guarantees
- Free trials with no credit card required
- "Cancel anytime"
- Social proof near the CTA (testimonials, review scores, customer count)
- Clear expectations of what happens next ("You'll get instant access to...")
- Privacy assurances

**How to execute:**
1. Focus on sections near CTAs (buttons, links, form submit areas)
2. List every reason someone might hesitate at that moment
3. Check if the copy addresses each concern
4. Add risk reversals or trust signals as needed
5. Strengthen CTA copy to be specific and benefit-oriented

**After this pass:** Loop back through all previous passes one final time: Pass 5 (Emotion), Pass 4 (Proof), Pass 3 (Benefits), Pass 2 (Voice), Pass 1 (Clarity).

## Word-Level Quick Checks

After completing all six passes, do a final sweep for these common issues:

**Cut these weak words:**
- Very, really, extremely, incredibly (weak intensifiers)
- Just, actually, basically (filler)
- In order to (use "to")
- That (often unnecessary)
- Things, stuff (vague)

**Replace business jargon with plain language:**

| Jargon | Plain English |
|--------|---------------|
| Utilize | Use |
| Implement | Set up |
| Leverage | Use |
| Facilitate | Help |
| Innovative | New |
| Robust | Strong |
| Seamless | Smooth |
| Cutting-edge | Modern |
| Synergy | Teamwork |
| Paradigm shift | Big change |

**Fix sentence structure:**
- Passive voice → Active voice ("The report was generated" → "We generated the report")
- Nominalizations → Verbs ("make a decision" → "decide", "perform an analysis" → "analyze")
- Long sentences → Split into shorter ones (max 25 words per sentence)

## Output Format

Present your edits using this structure:

### 1. Quick Assessment
- **Goal:** [What this copy is trying to accomplish]
- **Current state:** [1-2 sentence summary of strengths and weaknesses]
- **Editing approach:** [Which passes will provide the most value]

### 2. Edited Copy
Present the full edited version with **tracked changes** shown inline:

```
~~Original text that was removed~~
**Replacement text that was added**
```

### 3. Edit Explanations
For each significant change, explain:
- **What changed:** [Brief description]
- **Why:** [The principle or problem this fixes]
- **Expected impact:** [How this improves clarity, conversion, or brand alignment]

Group explanations by pass (Clarity edits, Voice edits, Benefits edits, etc.) for easy review.

### 4. Key Improvements Summary
Bullet list of the top 5 changes and their impact:
- ✅ [Change] → [Impact]
- ✅ [Change] → [Impact]
- ✅ [Change] → [Impact]

### 5. Recommendations Beyond Copy
If you notice structural or strategic issues that copy editing alone can't fix:
- Missing sections (social proof, FAQ, pricing details)
- Layout issues (CTA not visible, wall of text)
- Strategic gaps (wrong audience, unclear value prop)

Suggest complementary skills: **page-cro** for structural changes, **copywriting** for sections that need rewriting, **marketing-psychology** for deeper persuasion.

---

## Quality Checks

Before presenting your edited copy, verify:

1. **Core message preserved** — Did the editing maintain the author's intent?
2. **Voice consistency** — Does it sound like one person wrote it?
3. **Every claim supported** — Are bold statements backed by proof?
4. **Benefits are clear** — Will a non-technical reader understand what's in it for them?
5. **Specific and concrete** — Did you replace vague language with numbers and examples?
6. **Emotionally resonant** — Does it make the reader feel something?
7. **CTAs are frictionless** — Is the next step crystal clear with no barriers?
8. **No jargon or complexity** — Can a middle schooler understand it?

If any check fails, loop back to the relevant pass and fix it before presenting.

## Examples

### Example 1: Landing Page Hero Section

**User request:** "Review my hero section — it's not converting well"

**Original copy:**
```
Revolutionize Your Workflow with AI-Powered Automation

Our cutting-edge platform leverages machine learning to optimize your business processes, enabling seamless integration with your existing tech stack. Trusted by innovative companies worldwide.

Learn More
```

**Context gathered:**
- Surface: SaaS landing page hero
- Goal: Get signups for free trial
- Issue: Unclear value prop, too generic
- Proof available: 847 customers, average 6-hour time savings per week

**Edited copy:**
```
~~Revolutionize Your Workflow with AI-Powered Automation~~
**Stop wasting 10 hours a week on manual data entry**

~~Our cutting-edge platform leverages machine learning to optimize your business processes, enabling seamless integration with your existing tech stack.~~
**Our AI watches how you work, then automates the repetitive parts — no coding required. Works with the tools you already use.**

~~Trusted by innovative companies worldwide.~~
**Join 847 teams saving 6+ hours every week**

~~Learn More~~
**Start your free trial — no credit card needed**
```

**Edit explanations:**

**Clarity (Pass 1):**
- Headline: Changed from vague "revolutionize" to specific pain point. More immediately understandable.
- Body: Removed jargon ("leverages machine learning", "optimize business processes") and replaced with plain language explanation of what it actually does.

**Benefits (Pass 3):**
- Headline: Added specific benefit (save 10 hours) instead of abstract "revolutionize"
- Body: Added "no coding required" to address common objection, explained "how" not just "what"

**Proof & Specificity (Pass 4):**
- Social proof: Changed "innovative companies worldwide" to concrete number "847 teams" with specific outcome "6+ hours every week"

**Remove Friction (Pass 6):**
- CTA: Changed "Learn More" to action-oriented "Start your free trial" with friction reducer "no credit card needed"

**Key improvements:**
- ✅ Headline targets specific pain point → Grabs attention from right audience
- ✅ Body explains "what + how + why" in plain language → Clarity for non-technical users
- ✅ Added specific numbers (847, 6 hours) → Credibility and tangible benefit
- ✅ CTA is clear and low-risk → Removes barrier to conversion

### Example 2: Email Subject Line & Opening

**User request:** "Edit this welcome email — open rates are low"

**Original copy:**
```
Subject: Welcome to [Product Name]!

Hi there,

We're excited to have you on board. Our platform offers a wide range of features designed to help you succeed. To get started, we recommend exploring the dashboard and checking out our comprehensive documentation.

Best,
The Team
```

**Context gathered:**
- Surface: Welcome email (first touch after signup)
- Goal: Drive activation (get them to complete setup)
- Issue: Generic, doesn't drive action
- Proof: Users who complete setup in first session have 3x retention

**Edited copy:**
```
~~Subject: Welcome to [Product Name]!~~
**Subject: Your account is ready — 5 minutes to your first automation**

~~Hi there,~~
**Hi [First Name],**

~~We're excited to have you on board. Our platform offers a wide range of features designed to help you succeed. To get started, we recommend exploring the dashboard and checking out our comprehensive documentation.~~
**You're 5 minutes away from automating your first workflow. Here's exactly what to do:**

**1. Connect your tools (2 min)** — We'll guide you through it  
**2. Choose a template (1 min)** — Pick from 50+ pre-built workflows  
**3. Turn it on (30 sec)** — Watch it run automatically  

**[Button: Start 5-minute setup]**

**Fun fact:** Teams who finish setup today are 3x more likely to still be using [Product] in 30 days. Let's get you there.

~~Best,  
The Team~~
**— [Founder Name]  
Founder, [Product]**
```

**Edit explanations:**

**Clarity (Pass 1):**
- Subject: Specific action + timeframe (5 minutes) vs vague "welcome"
- Body: Replaced abstract "wide range of features" with concrete 3-step process

**Benefits (Pass 3):**
- Opening: Lead with benefit ("automate your first workflow") not product ("platform offers features")
- Added "exactly what to do" to answer the reader's immediate question

**Proof & Specificity (Pass 4):**
- Added "50+ pre-built workflows" (specific number)
- Added "3x more likely" stat to create urgency

**Emotional Resonance (Pass 5):**
- "Fun fact" framing makes the stat feel helpful, not pushy
- "Let's get you there" builds partnership tone

**Remove Friction (Pass 6):**
- Button with specific action "Start 5-minute setup" vs generic "explore dashboard"
- Time estimates for each step reduce overwhelm

**Voice (Pass 2):**
- Signed by founder (personal) vs "The Team" (corporate)

**Key improvements:**
- ✅ Subject line promises specific outcome + time investment → Higher open rate
- ✅ Body gives clear next steps → Reduces activation friction
- ✅ Added social proof ("3x more likely") → Creates urgency
- ✅ Personal sign-off from founder → Builds connection
